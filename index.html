Executive Summary

A Strong Foundation — Building the Next Phase of Growth

Six Peak Capital enters Q2 FY2026 from a position of strength: 11 active construction projects generating $6.4M in revenue and $1.7M in consolidated net income. The GC engine is running, the pipeline is full, and the team is executing.

As a general contractor, project-to-project revenue transitions are a normal part of the business cycle. The current awarded portfolio naturally rolls off — 5 of 11 projects complete before FY2028 — which is expected and creates the runway to focus business development efforts on sourcing and winning new projects for 2028 and 2029 award. Under the Base Case (A), FY2027 still delivers $2.3M in NI, with FY2028 reflecting the transition period at $-5K before newly awarded work ramps up.

Under the current interest rate and tax credit environment, the Steyn cost of capital — and any equivalent alternative capital source — captures most of the initial distributions from any LIHTC project, not including construction revenue. Essentially the LIHTC strategy is just a conduit for GC revenue prior to 2033, and given that, it makes sense to invest more into being a GC without all the other problems of LIHTC development, at least until capital markets change materially.

Sustainability Levers — Maintaining Revenue Through the Transition

The primary revenue engine is and will continue to be GC projects and non-LIHTC opportunities like Klump & Scott and the Uplifters development fee program. This portal models 7 scenarios across four levers that sustain the business through the project transition cycle. Each is independent and combinable:

1. Uplifters Foundation

60-property development fee program led by Schuyler Dietz. No capital required. 10% SP overhead ($150K/yr cap), tiered bonus (40/45/50%). Adds $2.4M to 3-year NI. In Scenarios B, C, D, E.

2. Klump & Scott

Two Steyn-guaranteed non-LIHTC developer deals. $1.5M pref equity from Steyn — 15% cost of capital passed to the deal. Add $1.8M to 3-year NI. In Scenarios C, D, F, G.

3. HVN Pipeline

2 Round 2 + 1 Round 3 future deals. No capital required. Extends revenue runway into FY2028–29. Adds $2.4M to 3-year NI (incremental over C). In Scenarios D, E, G.

4. Personnel Optimization

Base Case runs 2 fewer site superintendents. Klump/Scott scenarios restore full personnel. Already reflected in each scenario's expense structure.

Business Development — the core priority. Source and win new GC projects for 2028–2029 award to sustain the construction pipeline long-term.
