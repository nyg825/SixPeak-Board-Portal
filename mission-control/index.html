The Opportunity

Construction is a $2 trillion industry running on spreadsheets and phone calls.

LV Construction builds 30–200 unit multifamily projects (Type IIIA–V) across Los Angeles. The Extended BD plan commits a $402M pipeline — 25 deals over 5 years, signed bimonthly from October 2026 through April 2031. Led by Tom Taggart under partner oversight from Chris Aiello and Bob Kennedy. We’re profitable at every level. But we’re limited by how many projects our people can coordinate manually.

The Problem

90% of project management isn’t building—it’s coordination.

RFIs drafted by hand. Draw packages assembled over days. Cost data that’s a month old. Schedules updated weekly that are wrong by Tuesday. Subcontractor insurance tracked in spreadsheets. Every PM spends half their day on tasks a machine should handle.

Mission Control

An AI operating system built for construction.

Six modules. One platform. Trained on our own project data. Document intelligence that drafts RFIs in minutes. Real-time cost tracking tied to field progress. Automated draw packages, compliance monitoring, and scheduling—all from a single source of truth.

How It Works

Field data in. Intelligence out.

Superintendents capture progress with three taps and a photo. The platform does the rest: updates the schedule, flags risks, drafts documents, generates reports, verifies compliance, and shows every project’s status in real time. The CEO manages 12 projects from a dashboard, not from the driver’s seat.

The Scaling Mechanism

Add a project. Hire onsite staff. That’s it.

Mission Control automates 90% of the non-site work. Adding a new project requires only on-site staffing—a superintendent and a field engineer. The PMs, coordinators, accounting team, and office infrastructure absorb the incremental back-office load entirely through the platform. No new PM hire. No new coordinator. No additional accounting staff.

The Extended BD plan peaks at approximately 10–11 concurrent projects in FY2031–32. The 7 project-triggered hires (Francis APM, Denny/Lex/Acama Asst Supers, Acama Sr Super and Laborer, Reseda Sr Super) cover existing-portfolio coordination through FY2027. Mission Control lets each of those new staff serve more concurrent projects without proliferating the next wave of back-office hires. The platform does the coordination work that would otherwise consume half of every PM’s day—drafting documents, tracking costs, managing schedules—at machine speed.

The Financials

The Extended BD plan is the juice. Mission Control is the multiplier.

The Extended BD plan—25 deals, $402M aggregate GMP, bimonthly signings Oct 2026 – Apr 2031—generates $25.7M in cumulative consolidated net income through FY2033 (Base Case + Uplifters + Extended BD). That pipeline is the revenue engine. It funds the Mission Control buildout and justifies the investment.

Mission Control layers on top: a ~150 bps margin expansion on the $402M of GMP from cost-control automation, faster draws, and reduced change-order error—plus ~$1.5M from deferred next-wave hiring as the platform absorbs incremental coordination work. Total uplift: ~$7.5M additional NI over the plan horizon, taking cumulative NI to ~$33M.

The Competitive Advantage

Algorithms can be copied. Data can’t.

Every project on the platform trains the AI on our specific operation—our subs, our schedules, our cost patterns, our building types. Over the 5-year Extended BD rollout, 25 signed deals and ~10–11 peak concurrent projects feed a proprietary intelligence layer that no competitor can purchase. Off-the-shelf AI gives you generic. Mission Control gives you specific.

78% of contractors are testing AI. Less than 2% have deployed it. The window is now.
