Executive Summary

A Strong Foundation — Building the Next Phase of Growth

Six Peak Capital enters Q2 FY2026 from a position of strength: 11 active construction projects generating $6.4M in revenue and $1.7M in consolidated net income. The GC engine is running, the pipeline is full, and the team is executing.

As a general contractor, project-to-project revenue transitions are a normal part of the business cycle. The current awarded portfolio naturally rolls off — 5 of 11 projects complete before FY2028 — which is expected and creates the runway to focus business development efforts on sourcing and winning new projects for 2028 and 2029 award. Under the Base Case, FY2027 still delivers $2.3M in NI, with FY2028 reflecting the transition period at $-5K before newly awarded work ramps up.

Under the current interest rate and tax credit environment, the Steyn cost of capital — and any equivalent alternative capital source — captures most of the initial distributions from any LIHTC project, not including construction revenue. Essentially the LIHTC strategy is just a conduit for GC revenue prior to 2033, and given that, it makes sense to invest more into being a GC without all the other problems of LIHTC development, at least until capital markets change materially.

Sustainability Levers — Maintaining Revenue Through the Transition

The primary revenue engine is and will continue to be GC projects and non-LIHTC opportunities like Klump & Scott and the Uplifters development fee program. This portal models 5 plans across three levers that sustain the business through the project transition cycle. Each is independent and combinable:

1. Uplifters Foundation

60-property development fee program led by Schuyler Dietz. No capital required. 10% SP overhead ($150K/yr cap), tiered bonus (40/45/50%). Adds $2.4M to 3-year NI. In Base Case + Uplifters and Base Case + Uplifters + Extended BD.

2. Klump & Scott

Two Steyn-guaranteed non-LIHTC developer deals. $1.5M pref equity from Steyn — 15% cost of capital passed to the deal. Adds $1.8M to 3-year NI. In Base Case + Klump/Scott.

3. Extended BD Pipeline

30-deal third-party GC pipeline led by Tom Taggart with partner oversight from Chris Aiello and Bob Kennedy. $15–18M GMP each, bimonthly signings Oct 2026 – Apr 2032. Bonding revenue and costs assumed neutral, ~10.28% marginal NI. Generates $32.1M cumulative NI through FY2033 when combined with Uplifters — a $49.1M swing vs. Base Case alone.
